# TechSight Lead Gen Plan — M&A Partnership Focus

**Effective:** July 2026. This plan replaces the AI-Readiness/CTO-targeted lead gen. All marketing and lead gen now targets **M&A entities** with the goal of standing technical-diligence partnerships and upside participation (fee-plus-equity, co-investment, technical operating partner roles).

**The core insight:** Don't sell one diligence project to one buyer. Partner with entities that do multiple deals per year. One PE relationship = 3–10 diligence engagements/year plus post-close portfolio work plus equity opportunities. The sales motion is partnership development, not project sales.

---

## 1. Target Segments (Priority Order)

### Tier 1 — Search Funds & Independent Sponsors
- **Why first:** Easiest to reach, most underserved, most open to creative structures. Searchers have thin budgets (great fit for fee-plus-equity), no in-house technical capability, and their communities are concentrated and accessible. An independent sponsor doing 1–2 deals/year genuinely needs a technical partner, not a vendor.
- **Where:** SearchFunder.com (join, post, answer questions), Stanford/IESE search fund community and conferences, ETA (Entrepreneurship Through Acquisition) programs at MBA schools, independent sponsor conferences (iGlobal, DealMAX), Searchfunder/ETA podcasts as a guest.
- **Offer:** Free red-flag review on their first pipeline deal. Fee-plus-equity standard offer: reduced cash fee in exchange for 0.5–2% equity or an advisor stake. "Searcher package" pricing.

### Tier 2 — Family Offices
- **Why:** Already validated — the two strongest LinkedIn posts (Family Office Blind Spots, Code That Works vs. Scales) targeted this audience. Direct investors without technical staff; relationship-driven; longer hold periods mean bigger post-close upside.
- **Where:** Family office networks (FOX, Campden), direct-investing family office conferences, wealth advisor referrals, LinkedIn outreach to family office investment directors with tech/SaaS allocations.
- **Offer:** Standing diligence relationship + portfolio technical oversight ("your on-call CTO across the portfolio"). Co-investment offers where appropriate.

### Tier 3 — Lower-Middle-Market PE Firms ($10M–$250M deals, software/tech-enabled theses)
- **Why:** Highest deal velocity and repeat volume; hardest to win (established diligence panels — Crosslake, West Monroe, etc. serve upper MM). Wedge: faster turnaround, code-first depth (big firms interview; we read code), and willingness to take the small deals the big shops price out.
- **Where:** Axial (join as due diligence provider), operating partner networks, BDO/audit-firm referral relationships, warm intros via portfolio company CTOs, DealMAX/ACG events and local ACG chapter membership.
- **Offer:** Deal-flow retainer: pre-negotiated per-deal pricing + free red-flag screens on pipeline + priority scheduling. Post-close value-creation menu for the portfolio.

### Tier 4 — M&A Advisors, Brokers & Investment Banks (sell-side referral channel)
- **Why:** They don't buy diligence, but they control deal flow in both directions: sell-side exit-readiness engagements for their clients, and referrals to the buyers in their network. One good broker relationship = recurring referrals.
- **Where:** Axial, FE International / Quiet Light / Empire Flippers (for smaller SaaS deals), boutique software M&A banks (e.g., Software Equity Group tier), local business brokers handling tech-enabled companies.
- **Offer:** Referral arrangement + white-label exit-readiness reviews they can offer their sell-side clients ("get your client's tech story diligence-proof before going to market"). Their incentive: deals that don't blow up in buyer diligence close faster and at better multiples.

## 2. The Partnership Offer Ladder

Every relationship walks up this ladder:

1. **Free red-flag screen** (entry point, ~2–4 hours of work): quick technical read on a pipeline deal; deliver a 2-page memo. Proves the value in one deal cycle.
2. **Per-deal fixed-fee diligence** (core revenue): 2–3 week engagement, costed risk register.
3. **Deal-flow retainer**: pre-negotiated terms, free screens, priority turnaround for firms doing 3+ deals/year.
4. **Post-close work**: first-100-days plan, integration, portfolio fractional CTO — recurring revenue per portfolio company.
5. **Upside participation** (the goal): fee-plus-equity on diligence, co-investment alongside the sponsor, technical operating partner through the hold with carry/equity. Positioning line: *"When we say the platform supports your thesis, we're willing to bet on it with you."*

Rule of thumb for equity asks: searchers/independent sponsors — trade up to 50% of cash fee for equity; PE/family office — keep full fee on diligence, take equity/carry on post-close operating roles.

## 3. Channels & Motions

### A. Direct outreach (primary motion)
- Build a target list: 50 search funds (SearchFunder directory), 50 family offices with direct tech investments, 50 LMM PE firms with software theses (Axial, PitchBook if budget allows, PE firm websites), 30 software-focused M&A advisors.
- Sequence: see `outbound-emails-ma-partners.md` (three tracks: PE/sponsor, family office, M&A advisor).
- Volume target: 25 new contacts/week, personalized. Track in CRM with segment + deal-stage fields.

### B. LinkedIn content (air cover, 2 posts/week)
- **Pivot the calendar entirely to M&A topics.** The published M&A posts already outperformed — double down. Retire the AI-readiness/custom-ERP drafts or park them.
- Content pillars: (1) diligence war stories with dollar outcomes ("the $400K rewrite that changed deal economics"), (2) frameworks deal teams can steal (red-flag checklists, question lists for ICs), (3) sell-side/exit readiness for advisors, (4) post-close first-100-days lessons, (5) the incentive-alignment argument (why your diligence vendor should have skin in the game).
- Every post CTA → free red-flag screen or the diligence checklist lead magnet.
- Engage daily in comments of PE/ETA/search-fund voices (this is where searchers and sponsors actually hang out).

### C. Lead magnet (replace the AI Readiness Scorecard as primary)
- **"Technical Due Diligence Red-Flag Checklist for Software Acquisitions"** — a 2–3 page PDF/web checklist an IC member or searcher can run in an hour: multi-tenancy, key-person risk, DR, integration debt, AI claims, security posture. Gated by email OR ungated with a booking CTA (match the current no-gate philosophy).
- Second magnet for advisors: **"The Sell-Side Technical Story: 10 Things Buyers' Diligence Teams Look For."**
- The existing AI scorecard stays live (it feeds "AI claims diligence") but comes off the main nav funnel.

### D. Marketplaces & directories (set-and-forget pipeline)
- Axial due-diligence provider profile; SearchFunder vendor listing; ETA program vendor lists; Catalant/Graphite for one-off diligence gigs that can convert to relationships.

### E. Referral & partnership network
- CX.dev: keep the standing referral loop (they find execution problems → TechSight; we find governance gaps → CX.dev). Add: their clients getting acquired/acquiring = diligence referrals.
- Fractional CFO networks and quality-of-earnings shops (they're in every deal before we are — natural bundle: QoE + TechDD).
- M&A attorneys handling LMM software deals.
- Offer 10% referral fee where relationships are transactional; reciprocity where they're strategic.

### F. Speaking & community (slow burn, high trust)
- Guest spots: search fund/ETA podcasts, PE operating-partner podcasts.
- Talk title: "What Financial Diligence Misses: Reading the Code Behind the Deal."
- ACG chapter events; MBA ETA classes (searchers remember who taught them diligence).
