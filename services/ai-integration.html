The Challenge

Why AI Integration Is Harder Than It Looks

Calling an API is easy. Building a reliable, cost-effective AI feature that handles edge cases, scales with your user base, and doesn't surprise your customers — that's the hard part.

The Demo Trap

AI demos are impressive. AI in production is a different beast. Handling malformed inputs, rate limits, model failures, and unexpected outputs requires engineering discipline that most tutorials skip entirely.

Cost at Scale

A feature that costs $0.02 per request in testing can become a significant line item at thousands of daily active users. Without token management, caching, and cost controls, AI features can quietly erode margins.

Quality & Consistency

AI outputs are non-deterministic. Delivering a consistent user experience requires evaluation frameworks, output validation, fallback strategies, and ongoing monitoring — not just a prompt.
